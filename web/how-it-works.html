How it works

A grounded pipeline for a risky domain.

Pharos is designed so the medical fact comes from a dataset, not from model memory. QVAC turns the phone into the private inference surface around that grounded lookup.

Camera OCR

QVAC OCR reads the medication label from the phone camera capture.

Name resolution

The app normalizes brand text and synonyms into generic drug names.

DDInter lookup

The normalized scan is compared against shelf drugs in the local interaction database.

MedPsy explanation

QVAC MedPsy explains the retrieved fact and cites the source boundary.

Design reason

Medical AI needs retrieval before reasoning.

The app separates “what facts exist?” from “how do we explain those facts?” That lets the model add language clarity without becoming the authority for the interaction itself.

  • OCR and model explanation use QVAC.
  • Interaction facts come from DDInter 2.0.
  • The saved shelf remains private on the device.
  • The result includes a source and a medical disclaimer.

Failure modes

Refusal is a feature.

The user experience is built around safe stops. If Pharos cannot resolve the label or cannot ground the pair, the correct product behavior is to say so.

Low confidence

When OCR does not produce a usable medication name, the app asks for a better scan instead of guessing.

No dataset match

No documented match is presented as an absence of retrieved evidence, not proof of safety.

No anchor

If the mesh anchor is unavailable, the solo on-device path remains the base product experience.
